Digital design is an umbrella term that covers several different roles. Yes, each of these jobs involves design—and specifically designing what people see on their screen by way of websites or mobile apps—but the most common “digital design” roles focus on different elements. The brilliant thing about digital design is that once we are able to learned the best practices, we will start to see how they function across roles. It’s sort of like realizing that when we are fluent in a couple of romance languages, you can pick up Spanish or Italian or Portuguese. The other bonus of understanding digital design more generally? We will be able to better communicate with tech teams, seeing all sides of a project.

To give a thumbnail, basic digital design skills and tools include things like:

  • Color Theory
  • Typography
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Adobe InDesign
  • Figma
